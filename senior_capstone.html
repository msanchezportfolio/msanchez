Automated Storage & Dispnesing System

Designed for the Gazzola Biology Research Lab at UIUC, this project is an autonomous mechatronic system that stores liquid cell culture media at a constant 4°C and precisely dispenses volumes between 0.5mL and 50mL into a sterile reservoir for integration with an OT-2 liquid handler.

Three Sub-Systems

    Refrigeration: The thermal control system is engineered to maintain a 4°C environment to preserve the biological integrity of six 500mL liquid cell culture media bottles. This is achieved through several 12V, 60W Peltier cooling assemblies integrated with heat sinks and fans to facilitate heat transfer out of the unit. The unit is encased in 1"-thick polyisocyanurate (PIR) insulation, which effectively minimizes thermal leakage and allows the system to reach the target setpoint within 25 minutes from a room temperature of 22°C.​

    Pump: The dispensing subsystem utilizes a 12V peristaltic pump integrated with solenoid valves to ensure precise, leak-proof fluid handling. The peristaltic pump prevents cross-contamination by keeping the media entirely within medical-grade tubing, while the solenoid valves act as "gates" for the separate bottles of cell culture media. Driven by an Arduino-controlled relay, the pump provides a consistent flow rate and bi-directional fluid motion, allowing for the automation of media dispensing.

    Reservoir: The reservoir subsystem is a custom 3D-printed barrel designed to rotate mechatronically to capture and store cell culture within the lab's OT-2 Liquid Handler.
