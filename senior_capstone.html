Automated Storage & Dispnesing System

Designed for the Gazzola Biology Research Lab at UIUC, this project is an autonomous mechatronic system that stores liquid cell culture media at a constant 4°C and precisely dispenses volumes between 0.5mL and 50mL into a sterile reservoir for integration with an OT-2 liquid handler.

Mechanical Design & CAD Development

  • System Architecture: Led the CAD development (Autodesk Fusion 360) and physical assembly of a prototypes and final product using 3D printers, waterjet cutters, chopsaws, and laser cutters.
  • Thermal Engineering: Designed a refrigeration unit utilizing Peltier modules and aluminum cold plates to maintain the cell culture media at a constant 4°C
  • Structural Analysis: Validated frame integrity through deflection calculations for structural beams and performed static tipping simulations to ensure equipment safety. [cite: 328, 334, 335]
  • Precision Fluidics: Engineered a dispensing system capable of delivering precise volumes between 0.5 mL and 50 mL to an OT-2 liquid handler. [cite: 50, 52, 149]

Prototyping & Technical Validation

  • Iterative Prototyping: Manufactured multiple iterations using 5052-H32 sheet metal, T-slot framing, and water-jet cut acrylic. [cite: 66, 67, 105, 107, 108, 112, 114]
  • Performance Testing: Conducted thermal trials to confirm sub-zero cold side capabilities and verified a smooth cooling trend across prototypes. [cite: 83, 85, 349, 359]
  • Problem Solving: Analyzed experimental flow rates against theoretical values to identify and resolve motor stalling and tube compression issues. [cite: 399, 402, 403, 409, 547]
  • BOM Management: Managed a project budget of $1,859, overseeing procurement for over 60 line items including sensors, actuators, and custom fabricated parts. [cite: 204]
